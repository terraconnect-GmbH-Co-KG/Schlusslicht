Essay 01

The Inheritance of Merit

The concept of performance is often framed as a neutral metric of individual worth, yet it collapses when confronted with the reality of inherited advantage. Those who claim to have earned their status rarely account for the capital, education, and social networks handed down to them before they ever took a step.

Merit is the myth that legitimizes inheritance.

A system that equates starting points with outcomes obscures the structural violence of unequal distribution. True justice requires dismantling the assumption that birthright is a valid form of performance.

We must redefine achievement not as personal glory but as the collective capacity to overcome the weight of the past.

Lines of thought: Marx, inheritance critique, systemic inequality

Essay 02

Tools That Rule Us

Technology promises liberation but frequently delivers a new form of domination by embedding control into the very fabric of daily life. The tools we build to serve us often evolve into systems that dictate our behavior, limiting human agency to the parameters of the machine.

We do not master our tools; our tools master us.

This shift from instrument to master reflects a deeper failure to question the logic of efficiency that drives technological development. Herrschaft (domination) is no longer imposed by a tyrant but by the invisible code of the algorithm.

Freedom demands a rebellion against the technological imperative, prioritizing human values over automated optimization.

Lines of thought: Heidegger, Foucault, technological determinism

Essay 03

The School of Conformity

Education is frequently marketed as the path to freedom, yet in practice, it often functions as a mechanism for social adaptation and compliance. Schools teach students to fit into the existing economic order rather than to question its foundations or imagine alternatives.

Education either chains the mind or breaks the cage.

When the curriculum prioritizes efficiency and obedience over critical inquiry, it becomes an instrument of the status quo. True Bildung must be a process of awakening, not a training ground for the workforce.

We need an education that empowers individuals to dismantle the structures that confine them, not ones that reinforce them.

Lines of thought: Dewey, Gramsci, critical pedagogy

Essay 04

The Fear Mechanism

Fear is the most reliable tool of governance, used to justify the suspension of rights and the expansion of control. By manufacturing crises and amplifying threats, authorities create a population that is willing to trade liberty for the illusion of safety.

Fear is the shadow where power hides.

This manipulation transforms anxiety into a political resource, keeping citizens in a state of perpetual vulnerability. The ruling class thrives on the terror that the alternative is chaos, ensuring that no challenge to the system gains traction.

Overcoming this instrument of control requires the courage to face uncertainty without the promise of a guardian.

Lines of thought: Arendt, Hobbes, political psychology

Essay 05

Democracy Beyond the Ballot

The modern definition of democracy is dangerously narrow, reducing it to the periodic act of voting for representatives who often serve elite interests rather than the public. This ritual of selection masks the reality that true power remains concentrated outside the electoral sphere.

A vote is not a voice; it is a prayer.

Real democracy must extend into the workplaces, neighborhoods, and digital spaces where daily life is decided. It requires continuous participation and direct action, not merely the passive acceptance of a pre-packaged choice.

We must build a democracy that lives in the streets and the assemblies, not just in the polling station.

Lines of thought: Rousseau, anarchists, participatory democracy
