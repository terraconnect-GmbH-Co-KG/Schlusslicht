Essay 01

The Weight of Merit and Ancestry

Meritocracy promises a world where talent and effort alone determine one's fate, discarding the outdated privileges of wealth and class. In this ideal system, advancement is measured by performance, as demonstrated through examination or achievement, challenging hereditary aristocracy. Yet, the reality often reveals that inherited factors still heavily influence an individual's position in society, creating a gap between theory and practice. The notion that success reflects pure capability frequently masks the deep currents of nepotism and intergenerational privilege.

True merit is impossible when the starting line is drawn by one's ancestors.

Societal judgment often prioritizes fairness toward those who have benefitted from past struggles over fairness toward the beneficiaries of inheritance. This dilemma forces spectators to reconcile the logic of civic merit with the domestic reality of family gifts. People recast familial generosity as evidence of multigenerational striving, blurring the line between civic duty and private affection. The collision of these logics reveals how deeply the concept of merit is entwined with the weight of heritage.

Ultimately, the pursuit of a meritocratic society must acknowledge the heavy burden of ancestry that no policy can fully erase. Without addressing this, the promise of equality of opportunity remains a hollow ideal. We must rethink how we value effort when the path is paved by those who came before us. The weight of merit is inseparable from the weight of our inheritance.

Lines of thought: Meritocracy, Inherited Wealth, Intergenerational Privilege

Essay 02

Tools of Power and Domination

Technology is often celebrated as the great liberator, a force that dismantles traditional hierarchies and empowers the individual. In theory, tools should serve human needs, extending our capabilities and freeing us from the constraints of nature. Yet, history shows that technology frequently becomes a tool of power, reinforcing existing structures and creating new forms of domination. The very instruments designed to liberate us often become the mechanisms by which we are controlled.

The hammer that builds the cage is the same one that breaks the lock.

Modern systems of control rely on advanced technologies to monitor and regulate human behavior, creating a pervasive sense of surveillance. These tools do not merely observe; they actively shape our choices, limiting our autonomy and narrowing the scope of our freedom. The illusion of liberation is maintained while the reality of subjugation deepens, hidden behind the facade of progress. Technology, once a tool of human agency, has become the master of human will.

Ultimately, the pursuit of technological progress must confront the reality that tools can become the masters of human will. Without addressing this, the promise of liberation remains a hollow ideal. We must rethink how we value innovation when the path is paved by those who seek to control us. The weight of technology is inseparable from the weight of our domination.

Lines of thought: Technology, Domination, Surveillance
